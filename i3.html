Stage 3: Decay

The platform becomes a "useless pile of shit"

Terminal Stage

"The platform becomes a useless pile of shit. Surpluses are first directed to users; then, once they're locked in, surpluses go to suppliers; then once they're locked in, the surplus is handed to shareholders and the platform becomes a useless pile of shit. Then, they die."

The Endgame: All value is extracted from both user communities and business partners. The feed is clogged with ads, spam, and algorithmic noise. Authentic culture is buried. Users abandon the platform or adapt to its degraded state.

MySpace

Died from invasive ads and corporate takeover. Users fled to Facebook.

Tumblr

Porn ban killed community. Users migrated to Twitter and Discord.

Vine

Couldn't monetize 6-second videos. Creators left for YouTube.

Digg

Redesign prioritized publishers over users. Community died overnight.

Current Examples

  • Twitter/X: Algorithmic feed inserts tweets from people you don't follow, ads, and "suggested" content. The chronological feed is gone.
  • Instagram: Reels from strangers dominate the feed. Friends' photos are buried. The app is a TikTok clone.
  • Facebook: Over 30% of feed content comes from sources you never followed. It's a mall, not a social space.
  • YouTube: Search results favor monetized content. Creators chase algorithms, not passion.
