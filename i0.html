Who Owns the Feed?

Core: Platforms don’t just host culture—they feed on it.

Key Question: How do communities resist/adapt as platforms degrade through “enshittification”?

The Enshittification Lifecycle

“First, they are good to their users; then they abuse their users to make things better for their business customers; finally, they abuse those business customers to claw back all the value for themselves. Then, they die.” – Cory Doctorow

Doctorow outlines how platforms like Amazon, Facebook, and TikTok follow this pattern of user value → advertiser value → shareholder value → collapse.

Platform Vernaculars

André Brock shows how Black Twitter reshaped Twitter’s culture with unique hashtag practices and community discourse:

“Black Twitter is Twitter’s mediation of Black cultural discourse… a ‘social public’...” – Brock

This reflects how users can carve out cultural space even within exploitative systems.

AI Slop and Content Flood

John Oliver calls the rise of low-effort AI content the newest form of spam:

“We are going to be drowning in this shit for the foreseeable future.” – Oliver

Meta and TikTok push this slop via algorithms that prioritize engagement over quality.

Memetic Trends & Resistance

“Italian Brain Rot” memes illustrate how users weaponize absurdity and AI to create cultural vernaculars:

“It’s kind of turned into this big Internet collaboration.” – Hard Fork

These grassroots meme networks can subvert platform control, reclaiming attention and identity.

Algorithmic Feed Simulation

“TikTok couldn’t resist the temptation to show you the things it wants you to see rather than what you want to see.” – Doctorow
“That’s how slop sneaks in without your permission.” – Oliver
“Movements are making their own history, but in circumstances not entirely of their own choosing.” – Tufekci

Each quote is a snapshot in the feed of power, manipulation, and resistance.

External Control: The Cuban Twitter Project

“Later introduce political content aimed at inspiring Cubans to organize ‘smart mobs’...” – The Guardian

The U.S.-created “ZunZuneo” platform illustrates how feeds can be hijacked for covert influence.

Conclusion: Cultural Ownership

Users resist enshittification through memes, migrations, and alternate vernaculars. But the fight for the feed remains ongoing.

“The platform becomes a useless pile of shit.” – Doctorow
